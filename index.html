During the many countless hours I spent in the hospital throughout my childhood, I gained a deeper insight into healthcare as I watched my Egyptian immigrant grandmother fight with breast cancer. I always accompanied her to her appointments, doing my best to make her feel at ease in what was an intimidating environment for her. The discrimination she had faced as a Coptic Christian, a minority in Egypt persecuted for their beliefs, led to her doubting the healthcare system as a whole. In the patient room, I saw her struggle with the language barrier. While I helped as a translator, the fact that she could not directly understand the physicians made her cautious in taking their advice. She was not only in a foreign country with a different culture and set of ideals, but she felt vulnerable. She was physically vulnerable due to her ailment and mentally because she was entrusting a stranger with her life, a scenario that produced many complications back in Egypt because of her status as a Coptic Christian. After witnessing firsthand the discomfort my grandmother experienced in the healthcare setting, I pledged to make a difference to help immigrants facing similar struggles with acclimating successfully in foreign healthcare world by pursuing a career as a physician.
